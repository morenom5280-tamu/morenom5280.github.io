My name is Marisol and I’m interested in how people think, how technology communicates information, and how thoughtful design can make systems more inclusive and intuitive.

Designing With People in Mind

In Human Cognitive Processes, I learned how the human brain senses, perceives, and interprets information, which pushed me to think beyond functionality and focus on clarity, accessibility, and usability when creating interfaces. HCI gives me tools to think more intentionally about those moments where design meets real people.

Learning HCI in a Global Context

In CSCE 331, I gained hands-on experience working in a team to design a web application with accessibility in mind. The course emphasized designing for diverse users while maintaining a consistent brand identity through intentional color choices, typography, and cohesive styling across multiple interfaces. Studying HCI in Singapore feels especially meaningful because technology is shaped by culture, language, and environment. Being immersed in a new place challenges my assumptions and helps me think more carefully about who technology is built for.

Looking Ahead

As I move toward a career in software engineering, I want to build systems that are not only reliable and efficient, but also intuitive and accessible. HCI helps bridge the gap between technical depth and human experience, helping me create products that are human-centered, accessible, and responsive to the needs of people from different backgrounds.
